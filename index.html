Executive summary

India's fintech sector is transitioning from a growth-at-all-costs phase to a quality-and-profitability phase. Funding has normalised post-2021 peak, but early-stage activity surged 78% in H1 2025, indicating a new cohort of infrastructure and AI-native players entering. Lending tech has firmly displaced payments as the highest-conviction segment — capturing 36% of all deals and projected to generate $133B in revenue by 2030. The next battleground is embedded finance and AI-driven credit, where the convergence of Account Aggregator data rails, LLM-based underwriting, and UPI-linked repayment creates a structurally different credit model. Incumbents (banks, NBFCs) face a build-vs-partner dilemma as fintechs increasingly hold banking licences. Regulatory risk — particularly RBI's evolving stance on digital lending norms and UPI market cap — remains the single biggest variable.
